Why Glen Ellyn?

Known as the “Village of Volunteers,” Glen Ellyn is a charming, historic suburb with tree-lined streets, a walkable downtown, and strong community pride. It’s the kind of place where neighbors know each other, the Saturday farmers’ market is a tradition, and the local Fourth of July parade is not to be missed. For Brandon, who was born and raised here, Glen Ellyn isn’t just a market—it's home. His lifelong knowledge of the community gives his clients a true insider advantage.

Key Neighborhoods

Lake Ellyn Arboretum Estates Glen Ellyn Woods Raintree Ben Franklin Area
  • Lake Ellyn: Historic homes with character, steps from the lake and park.
  • Arboretum Estates: Spacious properties with a more secluded feel.
  • Raintree: Family-friendly with proximity to schools and parks.

Schools & Community Feel

Glen Ellyn’s schools are part of Glenbard Township High School District 87 and Glen Ellyn School Districts 41 and 89, all well-regarded. The village is also home to the College of DuPage, one of the largest community colleges in the country. The blend of excellent schools, cultural events, and community-driven activities makes it a top choice for families. (greatschools.org)

Home Market Snapshot

Median home prices hover in the mid-$500Ks, but historic properties near Lake Ellyn and newer custom builds can exceed $1M. The market is competitive, with demand driven by location, school quality, and the village’s timeless charm. (realtor.com)

Local Tips from Brandon

  • Don’t miss the French Market on Saturdays—it’s one of the best in the western suburbs.
  • Lake Ellyn Park is perfect year-round—boating in summer, ice skating in winter.
  • For a special night out, try dinner at Fire + Wine or a show at the McAninch Arts Center.
